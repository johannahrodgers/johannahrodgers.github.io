How To Play Games With Words: An Interactive Environment for Learning About the Standard Written English and the Parts of Speech

April, 2025

Johannah Rodgers

Overview

In both a real and metaphorical sense, the world we live in is made up of words. So why can’t we see words around us?  In Van C. Papa’s world, you can!  By playing with words and understanding their functions and relationships, learners will increase their understanding of some elements of Standard Written English as they interact with the parts of speech in this interactive multimedia environment.

Description

All of the parts of speech live together in a big house.  In the Standard Written English Dialect, the house is known as Van C. Papa’s House [verb, adjective, noun, conjunction, preposition, adverb, pronoun, article].  It is a house with lots of rooms and passageways.  However, it is only three stories high.  It is literally a house built out of words. Here are a few interesting things about the house: it is always getting bigger; you can create new rooms with new combinations of words; every room can be described with a different set of words and, as a result, may look different to different people.  

The living room is where all of the parts of speech congregate when they want to spend time together and show off their gymnastics; the dining room is where they eat together; and the bathroom is where they, of course, do not go altogether at one time, but each individually.  They also each have their own bedrooms, which are located on different floors based on their relationships to one another and their need to exit the front door quickly because of their frequency of use.   Although there are many doors in the house, only certain rooms are connected by a door.  You can keep track of all of the words you know and learn by putting them in places in the house.  

About the Characters

Nouns are fat and they eat things

Verbs are slippery.  They are shape shifters and they wiggle around

Adjectives admire themselves

Adverbs give commands in an indirect way

Prepositions are glue

Articles are either near sighted (the) or far sighted (a) and are always meant to specify

something

Punctuation marks do things!  In other words, they are not mere decoration, even though they may at time appear to be.

Pronouns are trading cards

Conjunctions are different kinds of bridges that are either open or closed or about to open or close.
